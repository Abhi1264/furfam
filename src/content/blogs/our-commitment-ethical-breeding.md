# Our Commitment to Ethical Breeding: The FurFam Promise (India)

FurFam exists to help **Indian families** bring dogs home **responsibly**—with eyes open to **health**, **behaviour**, **climate**, and **lifetime cost**. Ethical breeding is not a slogan; it is a **set of decisions** we take before a puppy meets your family.

## Health before hype

- **Parent health awareness**: screening and veterinary care appropriate to breed and context.  
- **Honest conversations** about risks—no breed is “perfect.”  
- **Documentation**: buyers deserve clarity, not marketing fog.

## Welfare over volume

We reject **puppy-mill economics**: cramped churn, minimal socialisation, and hidden medical issues hurt dogs and buyers alike. Partners must meet **care standards** we would accept for our own pets.

## Transparency builds trust

If you cannot ask hard questions, something is wrong. We encourage you to read [how our process works](/blogs/transparency-our-breeding-process) and to compare breeds using **data** on [/breeds](/breeds).

## Education is part of the sale

A puppy is not a product you “unbox.” We point new owners to:

- [First-time owner tips](/blogs/10-essential-tips-for-first-time-dog-owners)  
- [Nutrition basics](/blogs/nutrition-guide-healthy-dog-diet)  
- [Puppy proofing](/blogs/puppy-proofing-your-home-complete-guide)  
- [Vaccination overview](/blogs/puppy-vaccination-schedule-and-health-checks)

## India-specific responsibility

- **Heat safety** and realistic exercise plans.  
- **Society living** and barking management.  
- **Indie dogs** as valued companions—climate-resilient and deserving of ethical adoption.

## Your role as a buyer

- Never impulse-buy from roadside sellers.  
- Budget **vet care**, **training**, and **emergencies**.  
- **Spay/neuter** and population control decisions belong in your plan—discuss timing with your vet.

## Talk to us

[Contact FurFam](/contact) when you want guidance **without pressure**. We would rather help you **wait** than rush a mismatch.

Ethical breeding is **slow**, **boring**, and **kind**—exactly what dogs deserve.
