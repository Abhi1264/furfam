# How to Control Dog Shedding at Home in India

**Shedding** is normal—but in **Indian homes** with **tiles**, **AC**, and **compact spaces**, hair becomes a lifestyle issue fast.

This guide balances **coat science**, **humane grooming**, and **realistic cleaning**—without fake “stop shedding forever” promises.

## Understand the coat cycle

Dogs shed old hair to grow new hair. **Double-coated** breeds often “**blow coat**” seasonally—expect fluff mountains twice a year (timing varies by climate and indoor AC patterns).

## Tools that actually help

- **Slicker brush** for many medium/long coats
- **Undercoat rake** during heavy shed
- **Rubber curry** or **grooming gloves** for short coats

Always brush **before** baths—wet mats tighten painfully.

## Bathing and blow-dry

A **clean coat releases undercoat** more easily—but over-bathing dries skin. Follow [bathing frequency](/blogs/how-often-should-you-bathe-your-dog) guidance.

If you use a dryer, prefer **cool air** and avoid overheating—especially in summer.

## Nutrition shows in coat

Poor diets can cause **dull coat** and **excessive shedding**—but so can parasites and thyroid disease. If shedding comes with **weight changes** or **skin issues**, see your vet.

Read [nutrition basics](/blogs/nutrition-guide-healthy-dog-diet) and [skin problems](/blogs/common-dog-skin-problems-and-what-to-do).

## Home management (India-specific)

- **Washable sofa covers** beat fighting hair daily.
- **Outdoor brushing** on a balcony or terrace when safe—keeps AC filters cleaner.
- **Robot vacuums** help on tile; empty often during shed season.

## When shedding is a red flag

- **Bald patches**
- **Black specks** (possible flea dirt)
- **Crusty skin** or odor

Don’t mask symptoms with oils—diagnose first.
