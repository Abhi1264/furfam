# Dog Weight Management in India: How to Prevent Obesity

**Canine obesity** is one of the most common preventable diseases—and in **Indian cities**, where heat limits exercise and treats flow from family members, weight creeps up fast.

Keeping your dog **lean** improves **joint health**, **heat tolerance**, and **lifespan**. This guide gives a practical system any household can follow.

## Why obesity hurts more in hot climates

Extra weight increases:

- **Heat load** during walks
- **Joint stress** on stairs and tiles
- **Anesthesia risk** if surgery is ever needed

## Body condition: learn to score weekly

Stand above your dog:

- **Ribs**: palpable with slight fat cover—not invisible, not buried.
- **Waist**: visible taper behind ribs when viewed from above.
- **Abdomen**: tucked up when viewed from the side (breed variations exist).

Take a photo monthly—our eyes adjust slowly.

## Food: measure, don’t guess

1. Use a **kitchen scale** for kibble grams when possible.
2. Follow label starting points, then **adjust every 2–3 weeks** based on trend.
3. Read [food labels](/blogs/how-to-read-dog-food-labels-like-a-pro) so you understand calorie density.

## Treat culture in Indian homes

Relatives love feeding dogs—set **house rules**:

- One designated treat jar with **small low-calorie** options.
- **No feeding from the table** during festivals or gatherings.
- Swap food affection for **play and training** rewards.

## Exercise without heatstroke

Shift walks to **cool hours** and add **sniff games** indoors. See [exercise guide](/blogs/how-much-exercise-does-your-dog-really-need).

## When weight gain is not “just food”

Sudden gain, pot-bellied appearance, or coat changes can signal **hormonal disease**. Your vet may run blood tests before you cut calories further.

## Senior dogs

Metabolism shifts—pair diet changes with [senior care](/blogs/senior-dog-care-after-age-seven).
