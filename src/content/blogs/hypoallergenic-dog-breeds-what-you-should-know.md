# Hypoallergenic Dog Breeds in India: What You Should Know

“**Hypoallergenic**” is one of the most misunderstood labels in the pet world—especially in **Indian cities** where **dust**, **pollution**, and **humidity** already challenge sensitive airways.

This article explains what **science supports**, which breeds are **commonly tolerated**, and how to **reduce allergens** at home.

## What actually triggers allergies?

Most reactions involve:

- **Dander** (skin flakes)
- **Saliva** (licking spreads proteins)
- **Urine residue** on fur
- **Environmental pollen and dust** that clings to coats

A dog that sheds less may spread **less hair around the house**, but it is not automatically “safe.”

## Breeds often discussed for allergy-sensitive homes

### Poodle (Toy/Miniature/Standard)

Curly coat traps shed hair until grooming. Needs **regular professional grooming** in humid climates to avoid matting and skin issues.

### Bichon Frise and Maltese

Small, companion-oriented breeds with **high grooming needs**.

### Portuguese Water Dog

Active and curly-coated—great for engaged owners.

### Soft Coated Wheaten Terrier

Medium energy with a coat that needs consistent care.

Always **trial exposure**—visit the dog multiple times if possible.

## India-specific considerations

- **Humidity** increases skin infections if coats are not dried properly after baths.
- **Monsoon** brings mold and dust mites—clean filters and bedding more often.
- **AC** can dry skin; balance bathing frequency with vet advice.

## Home hygiene playbook

1. **Brush** on a schedule; do it outdoors when possible.
2. **Wash** dog beds weekly.
3. **HEPA** air purifier in main living areas.
4. **Hard floors** beat heavy carpets for dander control.
